# The Foster-to-Crime Pipeline

## The one-line thesis

America's foster care system isn't a safety net. For hundreds of thousands of children, it's a conveyor belt into prison, homelessness, early parenthood, and sex trafficking — and the Church has outsourced the entire operation to the state.

## 1. Foster care → prison

### The headline stat — the one that should end arguments

**Roughly 1 in 5 American prison inmates is a former foster child.** The Bureau of Justice Statistics found that former foster youth are overrepresented in the prison population by a factor of ~25x relative to the general population ([CEPR / Bald, Chyn, Humlum, Stephenson, 2022](https://cepr.org/voxeu/columns/foster-care-prison-pipeline)).

### The aging-out pipeline — the sharpest numbers

From the gold-standard **Midwest Evaluation of the Adult Functioning of Former Foster Youth** (Chapin Hall, University of Chicago — 732 youth followed from age 17 to 26 across IL, IA, WI):

- **~70% of former foster youth have been arrested at least once by age 26** — and for males that number climbs to nearly 81% ([The Imprint, citing Midwest Study](https://imprintnews.org/top-stories/decriminalize-young-people-who-grew-up-in-foster-care/246740); [Chapin Hall / PA Courts PDF](https://ocfcpacourts.us/wp-content/uploads/2020/06/Midwest_Evaluation_of_the_Adult_Functioning_001015.pdf))
- **~50% of transitioning foster youth experience arrest or incarceration** in the transition window alone ([The Imprint, 2023](https://imprintnews.org/top-stories/decriminalize-young-people-who-grew-up-in-foster-care/246740))
- **30% arrested, 15% convicted of a crime, and 29% incarcerated** in just the two years between Midwest Study interviews ([Chapin Hall Midwest Study PDF, p.18](https://ocfcpacourts.us/wp-content/uploads/2020/06/Midwest_Evaluation_of_the_Adult_Functioning_001015.pdf))
- **7% were currently incarcerated at interview** (14% of males, 1% of females) — i.e. **one in seven former foster boys was literally in a cell the day the survey reached them** ([Chapin Hall Midwest Study PDF](https://ocfcpacourts.us/wp-content/uploads/2020/06/Midwest_Evaluation_of_the_Adult_Functioning_001015.pdf))

### The incarceration rate is ~10x the general population

A peer-reviewed prospective study of WA state foster youth found **nearly 13% entered state prison between ages 18–23**. Compare that to the national baseline: **2.7% of males and 0.2% of females** ages 18–24 have ever served prison time ([Journal of Research in Crime and Delinquency / NIH PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8975219/)).

Broken out by exit type:

| Exit type | % who entered state prison by age 23 |
|---|---|
| Adopted | 3.2% |
| Guardianship / relative placement | 7.0% |
| Aged out | 8.6% |
| Reunified with family | 13.2% |
| AWOL / runaway | 19.1% |
| Exited to juvenile detention | **48.2%** |

Source: [Foster care, permanency, and risk of prison entry — NIH PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8975219/)

### And then the lifetime number

A 2025 longitudinal analysis in *Children and Youth Services Review* found **nearly 30% of foster care youth were incarcerated in young adulthood, with lifetime incarceration rates by age 20 reaching 42%** ([ScienceDirect, 2025](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0145213425002248)).

### Placement instability multiplies the risk

**A child who has moved through 5 or more placements has a 90% risk of criminal-legal system involvement.** By age 17, over 50% of foster children already have an encounter (arrest, conviction, or detention) with the juvenile legal system ([The Criminal Law Practitioner, American University](https://www.crimlawpractitioner.org/post/the-foster-care-to-prison-pipeline-a-road-to-incarceration)).

> **The indictment:** A child bounced through five homes is more likely to be arrested than a child born with a loaded gun in their crib. That's not a broken system — that's the system working as designed.

## 2. Foster care → homelessness

- **22–30% of youth who age out of foster care become homeless during the transition to adulthood**, compared to a **4% lifetime rate** in the general population ([Penny Lane, synthesizing 2024 research](https://www.pennylane.org/resources/foster-care-statistics); [American Journal of Public Health / NIH PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3969135/))
- **31–46% of aged-out foster youth have been homeless at least once by age 26** — the Midwest Study's confidence interval, depending on how unobserved cases are counted ([AJPH / NIH PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3969135/))
- **~29% of all unhoused youth ages 13–25 nationally have spent time in foster care** ([Alternative Family Services, synthesizing federal homeless-youth counts](https://www.afs4kids.org/blog/35-foster-youth-homelessness-statistics-you-should-know/))
- **In the San Francisco Bay Area, ~75% of unhoused youth were previously in foster care and/or the juvenile justice system** ([Alternative Family Services](https://www.afs4kids.org/blog/35-foster-youth-homelessness-statistics-you-should-know/))
- **61% of homeless former foster youth end up incarcerated**, vs. 46% of homeless youth with no welfare history — homelessness is itself a prison accelerant for this population ([Alternative Family Services](https://www.afs4kids.org/blog/35-foster-youth-homelessness-statistics-you-should-know/))

> **The indictment:** The American church funded a building program while nearly one-third of children raised by the state ended up sleeping on the street.

## 3. Foster care → sex trafficking

This is the statistic that should stop every sermon cold.

- **An estimated 60% of child sex trafficking victims recovered in a 2013 FBI nationwide raid across 70+ cities were children from foster care or group homes** ([Iowa HHS / Human Trafficking Safety of Children in Foster Care PDF](https://hhs.iowa.gov/media/6797); [Loyola Children's Legal Rights Journal PDF](https://lawecommons.luc.edu/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=1160&context=clrj))
- **The National Center for Missing & Exploited Children reported that 86% of likely child sex trafficking victims in 2016 were in the care of foster care or social services** at the time of their disappearance ([Loyola CLRJ PDF](https://lawecommons.luc.edu/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=1160&context=clrj))
- **In a Connecticut state study, 86 of 88 identified child sex trafficking victims had prior child welfare involvement** ([Loyola CLRJ PDF](https://lawecommons.luc.edu/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=1160&context=clrj))
- **Los Angeles Probation reported that 60% of juveniles arrested for prostitution were in the foster care system** ([Human Trafficking Search / City Limits synthesis](https://humantraffickingsearch.org/resource/37-foster-care-and-human-trafficking-statistics/); [Loyola CLRJ PDF](https://lawecommons.luc.edu/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=1160&context=clrj))
- **50–90% of child sex trafficking victims identified in 2013 had some prior involvement with child welfare services** ([Human Trafficking Search / Child Welfare Information Gateway](https://humantraffickingsearch.org/resource/37-foster-care-and-human-trafficking-statistics/))
- **In NYC, 75% of trafficked youth had spent time in foster care**; in CA, **50% of children sold into trafficking had previous foster care involvement** ([Human Trafficking Search / City Limits](https://humantraffickingsearch.org/resource/37-foster-care-and-human-trafficking-statistics/))

> **The indictment:** When the U.S. government wants to find a child to be raped for profit, the most efficient place to look is the foster system it itself operates. Every pastor who has ever preached against the sex trade but never called a foster agency is accountable to that fact.

## 4. Foster care → teen pregnancy (the intergenerational engine)

- **By age 21, 71% of young women who aged out of foster care had been pregnant at least once** — vs. 34% in the general population. That's more than double ([Midwest Evaluation / FosterVA synthesis](https://www.fosterva.org/blog/what-percentage-of-girls-in-foster-care-are-pregnant-by-21))
- **By age 19, 55% of aged-out foster females were already pregnant; 23% of aged-out males had fathered a child** — the pregnancy rate among foster youth **increased 300% between ages 17 and 19** ([Children and Youth Services Review / NIH PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3902972/))
- **At age 19, 50% of foster females have been pregnant vs. 20% of general-population females** — a 2.5x gap that appears regardless of maltreatment history, religiosity, school connectedness, or academic achievement ([Dworsky & Courtney / NIH PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3902972/); [Guttmacher Institute](https://www.guttmacher.org/gpr/2011/06/teen-pregnancy-among-young-women-foster-care-primer))
- **Teen parents in foster care are more likely to have their own children placed in foster care**, creating an intergenerational cycle the system euphemistically calls "continuity of involvement" ([Casey Family Programs, 2024](https://www.casey.org/pregnant-parenting-strategies/))

> **The indictment:** The foster system is manufacturing the next generation of foster children.

## 5. The taxpayer price tag

The Annie E. Casey Foundation — the country's leading foundation on foster outcomes — puts numbers on it:

- **Over a 10-year window, the ~300,000 youth who left foster care without successful transition support cost American society nearly a quarter of a trillion dollars** ([Annie E. Casey Foundation: Cost Avoidance](https://www.aecf.org/resources/cost-avoidance-the-business-case-for-investing-in-youth-aging-out-of-foster))
- **Justice-system costs alone: >$5 billion per annual aging-out cohort of young men** ([Annie E. Casey Foundation](https://www.aecf.org/resources/cost-avoidance-the-business-case-for-investing-in-youth-aging-out-of-foster))
- **Lost-earnings / high-school dropout costs: >$2 billion per annual cohort** (foster youth graduate HS by 19 at 58% vs. 87% in the general population) ([Annie E. Casey Foundation](https://www.aecf.org/resources/cost-avoidance-the-business-case-for-investing-in-youth-aging-out-of-foster))
- **Teen parenthood costs: ~$250 million per annual cohort of aged-out young women** ([Annie E. Casey Foundation](https://www.aecf.org/resources/cost-avoidance-the-business-case-for-investing-in-youth-aging-out-of-foster))

Extrapolated forward: **every year America fails to solve this, the bill is ~$7–8B in justice-system-only costs, and the kids pay the non-dollar portion in years of their lives.**

## 6. The causal question — does foster care *cause* the crime?

A 2022 NBER quasi-experimental study (using near-random assignment of child welfare investigators as an instrument) found that for the marginal child — those where one investigator would have placed them and another would not — **foster placement actually *reduced* the arrest rate by age 19 from 37% to 12%, conviction from 35% to 7%, and incarceration from 26% to 5%** ([CEPR / Bald, Chyn, Humlum, Stephenson 2022](https://cepr.org/voxeu/columns/foster-care-prison-pipeline); [r/science discussion of NBER preprint](https://www.reddit.com/r/science/comments/1luulpp/one_fifth_of_the_us_prison_population_are_former/)).

So:

1. Foster youth are catastrophically overrepresented in prison. That's observational fact.
2. For the *marginal* child, entering foster care *reduces* their crime risk versus being left in the abusive home.
3. **Which means:** the households these children came from were even worse than foster care — and the American church let those households exist, let those children enter state care, and then let state care hand them to prisons and pimps anyway.

There is no interpretation of the data under which the Church comes out looking responsible.
