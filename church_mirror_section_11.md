**Sexual abuse and mental-health collapse among American foster children**

## 11.1 The thesis

The pipeline in §10 described what foster care does to children downstream — prison, homelessness, trafficking, early parenthood. This section describes what foster care does to children *while they are still inside it.* The answer: it frequently recreates the wound it was built to heal. Foster children are sexually abused at multiples of the general-population rate, and the mental-health collapse that follows is so severe that foster alumni suffer PTSD at twice the rate of U.S. combat veterans.

The Church built a theology of the family. The state built a system of out-of-home placement. And the children — 329,000 of them tonight — live in the gap between the two.

## 11.3 Sexual abuse *inside* the system

### The multiplier

- **Johns Hopkins, Baltimore:** foster children are **4x more likely to be sexually abused** than children in the general population. In group homes, **28x more likely** ([NCCPR, citing Benedict & Zuravin, Johns Hopkins School of Hygiene and Public Health, 1992](https://nccpr.org/nccpr-issue-paper-1-foster-care-vs-family-preservation-the-track-record-for-safety-and-well-being/))
- **Indiana study (Spencer & Knudsen):** **3x more physical abuse and 2x more sexual abuse in foster homes** than the general population; in group homes, **10x more physical abuse and 28x more sexual abuse** ([NCCPR](https://nccpr.org/nccpr-issue-paper-1-foster-care-vs-family-preservation-the-track-record-for-safety-and-well-being/))
- **Leeds, UK retrospective:** foster children were **7–8x more likely** to be assessed for abuse than a child in the general population; foster parents were perpetrators in 41% of cases ([Hobbs, Hobbs & Wynne, 1999, via Illinois Children and Family Research Center](https://www.cfrc.illinois.edu/pubs/rp_20010501_ChildMaltreatmentInFosterCareAStudyOfRetrospectiveReporting.pdf))

### The self-report data — worse than official numbers by an order of magnitude

- **Casey Family Program alumni study:** **24% of girls reported actual or attempted sexual abuse** — and this counted only the one foster home the child had lived in longest. Any child moved *because* she was abused wouldn't be counted ([Fanshel et al., 1990, via NCCPR](https://nccpr.org/nccpr-issue-paper-1-foster-care-vs-family-preservation-the-track-record-for-safety-and-well-being/))
- **Oregon/Washington foster children:** **~1/3 reported being abused by a foster parent or another adult in a foster home**. The study didn't even ask about the most common form of abuse in foster care: foster children abusing each other ([NCCPR, citing Pecora et al.](https://nccpr.org/nccpr-issue-paper-1-foster-care-vs-family-preservation-the-track-record-for-safety-and-well-being/); [ResearchGate: Out-of-Home Maltreatment](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/222972786_Out-of-home_maltreatment_An_analysis_of_risk_in_various_settings_for_children))
- **Casey Northwest Alumni Study:** **~1/3 of alumni reported abuse by a foster parent or another adult in the foster home** ([NCCPR, 2005](https://nccpr.org/80-percent-falure-a-brief-analysis-of-the-casey-family-programs-northwest-foster-care-alumni-study/))
- **Baltimore case-records study:** **28% of foster homes studied had documented abuse** — more than 1 in 4 ([L.J. v. Massinga, via NCCPR](https://nccpr.org/nccpr-issue-paper-1-foster-care-vs-family-preservation-the-track-record-for-safety-and-well-being/))
- **Girls in foster care (retrospective review):** **81% had been sexually abused** (before and/or during care); 68% by more than one perpetrator; 95% had been neglected ([PubMed, 2009](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19550260/))
- **Lifetime prevalence of sexual abuse among foster youth: 27%** — at or near one in three ([Ahrens et al. 2012 via ScienceDirect, 2024](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0145213424000334))
- **Females in care: 19.5% reported at least one type of sexual abuse while in foster care; males: 10.4%** ([Ballard Brief, BYU, 2022](https://ballardbrief.byu.edu/issue-briefs/sexual-abuse-of-children-in-the-united-states-foster-care-system))

### Who does it

- **Casey / Fanshel alumni data, detailed case-record review (Baltimore, Zuravin & Benedict, 296 foster homes, 5-year period):** Of confirmed maltreatment cases, **48% were for sexual abuse**, and **the foster parent was the perpetrator in 64% of the sexual-abuse cases** ([Illinois CFRC / Tittle, Poertner, Garnier](https://www.cfrc.illinois.edu/pubs/rp_20010501_ChildMaltreatmentInFosterCareAStudyOfRetrospectiveReporting.pdf))
- **Foster parent perpetrators in non-related foster homes: 56%** of all maltreatment cases ([Illinois CFRC](https://www.cfrc.illinois.edu/pubs/rp_20010501_ChildMaltreatmentInFosterCareAStudyOfRetrospectiveReporting.pdf))
- **Child-on-child sexual abuse:** in one sample, **20% of sexual abuse in foster care was perpetrated by other foster children** ([Illinois CFRC](https://www.cfrc.illinois.edu/pubs/rp_20010501_ChildMaltreatmentInFosterCareAStudyOfRetrospectiveReporting.pdf))

### The official lie

States are required to report maltreatment rates. They routinely report **"99.9% of foster children are not maltreated"** — e.g. Florida's FY2020–FY2024 federal outcome reports show <0.1% maltreatment per year ([HHS Child Welfare Outcomes, Florida](https://cwoutcomes.acf.hhs.gov/cwodatasite/byState/florida/)).

That number is approximately 30–100x lower than what outside researchers find when they interview the children themselves ([NCCPR analysis, 2010](https://www.nccprblog.org/2010/05/most-common-lie-in-american-child.html)).

> **Indictment:** The federal reporting system is designed to protect the bureaucracy, not the child. The children who tell outside researchers the truth describe a system where 1 in 4 to 1 in 3 of them has been sexually assaulted — in the place they were sent *for their safety.*

### Prior trauma as a targeting signal

- **~30% of children entering foster care have already been sexually abused** before arrival ([Ballard Brief, BYU](https://ballardbrief.byu.edu/issue-briefs/sexual-abuse-of-children-in-the-united-states-foster-care-system))
- **85% of foster children have experienced trauma prior to entering care**; of those, **52% had been exposed to prior sexual abuse** ([North Carolina data via Ballard Brief](https://ballardbrief.byu.edu/issue-briefs/sexual-abuse-of-children-in-the-united-states-foster-care-system))
- **~50% of individuals sexually abused once are revictimized** in childhood or adolescence ([Ballard Brief](https://ballardbrief.byu.edu/issue-briefs/sexual-abuse-of-children-in-the-united-states-foster-care-system))
- Prisoners convicted of sex crimes, when asked directly, report **they specifically target "children who do not have many friends and who appear to lack confidence, to have low self-esteem, and to be unhappy and emotionally needy"** — a near-perfect description of a foster child mid-placement ([Ballard Brief](https://ballardbrief.byu.edu/issue-briefs/sexual-abuse-of-children-in-the-united-states-foster-care-system))

> **Indictment:** The foster child is pre-selected by predators. The system gathers these children into facilities, places them with strangers, moves them every few months — and then is shocked when 24% of the girls report sexual abuse.

## 11.4 The mental-health collapse

### Prevalence — the baseline horror

- **Up to ~80% of children in foster care have a significant behavioral or mental-health problem requiring intervention** — vs. ~20% in the general population ([NIH PMC: Mental Health Services for Children Placed in Foster Care](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3061347/); [Mental Health Virginia](https://mentalhealthvirginia.org/disparities-in-mental-health-care-among-virginias-foster-children/))
- **Foster children are 5x more likely to have anxiety and 7x more likely to have depression** than children outside the welfare system ([Mental Health Virginia](https://mentalhealthvirginia.org/disparities-in-mental-health-care-among-virginias-foster-children/))
- **63.3% of Casey adolescents (ages 14–17) had a lifetime DSM-IV diagnosis; 22.8% had three or more lifetime diagnoses** ([Casey Field Office Mental Health Study / NIH PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3061347/))
- **Foster adolescents vs. the general population (age-matched national comparison):**
  - Major depression: **19.0% vs. 11.9%**
  - PTSD: **13.4% vs. 5.2%**
  - Conduct disorder: **20.7% vs. 7.0%**
  - ADHD: **15.1% vs. 4.5%**
  - Source: [NIH PMC CFOMH Study](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3061347/)
- **Depression diagnosis rate: 23.6% of foster/adopted-from-foster children** vs. 2.5% in a biological two-parent home ([Ballard Brief, BYU](https://ballardbrief.byu.edu/issue-briefs/sexual-abuse-of-children-in-the-united-states-foster-care-system))

### PTSD: double combat veterans

The **Casey Family Programs / Harvard Medical School / University of Michigan Northwest Alumni Study** (659 Washington and Oregon foster alumni) is the gold standard:

- **Former foster children are ~2x more likely to suffer from PTSD than Iraq War veterans** ([The Harvard Crimson, 2005](https://www.thecrimson.com/article/2005/4/11/study-finds-foster-kids-suffer-ptsd/); [Seattle Times](https://www.seattletimes.com/seattle-news/study-ex-foster-children-face-big-hurdles/))
- **25% of alumni had PTSD in the previous 12 months** ([Family Preservation Foundation / Casey](https://familypreservationfoundation.org/more/blog/one-in-four-foster-children-suffers-from-post-traumatic-stress-study-finds))
- **54% of alumni reported at least one mental-health issue**; **54.4% had at least one lifetime CIDI DSM-IV diagnosis** ([NIH PMC, Casey NWAS](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3061347/))
- **Former foster alumni are 2x as likely to be depressed (20%) as the general population (10%)** ([Family Preservation Foundation](https://familypreservationfoundation.org/more/blog/one-in-four-foster-children-suffers-from-post-traumatic-stress-study-finds))
- **Among sexually abused foster children specifically: PTSD diagnosis rate was 60%**; among physically abused foster children, 42% ([Dubner & Motta, 1999, via NIH PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3061347/))
- **98% of foster alumni with depression had comorbid mental illness; 94% had comorbid PTSD** ([Jackson 2008, Casey National Alumni Study](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3061347/))

> **Indictment:** We send 19-year-olds to war and call it service. We send 3-year-olds to foster care and call it care. The 3-year-olds come home more traumatized.

### Suicide: the numbers no one wants to cite

The CalYOUTH Study (California Youth Transitions to Adulthood Study) is the largest contemporary dataset on foster-youth suicidality:

- **40.9% of 17-year-olds in California foster care reported suicidal ideation; 23.5% reported a suicide attempt by age 17** ([Courtney et al., CalYOUTH, via NIH PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9838394/))
- **General-population comparison (2020):** 12% of adolescents reported suicidal thoughts; **2.5% reported a suicide attempt** ([SAMHSA 2021 via NIH PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9838394/))
- → Foster youth are **~3.4x more likely to think about suicide and ~9.4x more likely to have attempted it** by 17
- **Foster children are ~4x more likely than other children to attempt suicide** ([The Imprint, 2020](https://imprintnews.org/childrens-mental-health/suicide-looms-large-minds-many-foster-youth/47755))
- **Among 9-to-11-year-olds in Denver-area foster care, >25% had a history of suicidality** (thoughts or behaviors) ([Taussig, University of Denver via The Imprint](https://imprintnews.org/childrens-mental-health/suicide-looms-large-minds-many-foster-youth/47755))
- **>50% of CalYOUTH 17-year-olds had a current positive diagnosis for one or more mental/behavioral health disorder** ([Chapin Hall, 2022](https://www.chapinhall.org/research/prevalence-of-mental-health-disorders-suggests-youth-reaching-age-of-majority-in-foster-care-need-special-attention/))
- **Former foster alumni at twice the rate of mental illness of general population; overall foster care fails for ~80% of alumni** ([NCCPR / Casey NWAS synthesis](https://nccpr.org/80-percent-falure-a-brief-analysis-of-the-casey-family-programs-northwest-foster-care-alumni-study/))

> **Indictment:** One in four California 17-year-olds in foster care has already tried to kill themselves. The Church is arguing about whether to modernize the sanctuary lighting.

### The systemic failure to treat

- In 2003, **over 90% of child-welfare agencies systematically assessed physical health, but fewer than 50% assessed mental health** ([Leslie et al. via NIH PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9838394/))
- **Screening of 9-to-11-year-olds for mental health generally doesn't happen unless they already show major behavioral issues** ([Taussig via The Imprint](https://imprintnews.org/childrens-mental-health/suicide-looms-large-minds-many-foster-youth/47755))
- Alumni studies document "long delays between illness onset and diagnosis and treatment" for PTSD, anxiety, and depression — leading to **2-to-7-fold increases in risk of adult mental-health disorder among alumni** ([NIH PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3061347/))
- **Only 20% of Casey Northwest alumni could be classified as "doing well."** Even if every fixable policy problem were magically solved, that number would only reach **22.2%** ([NCCPR / Casey NWAS](https://nccpr.org/80-percent-falure-a-brief-analysis-of-the-casey-family-programs-northwest-foster-care-alumni-study/))

## 11.5 The chain reaction — how §11 produces §10

The numbers in this section don't sit in isolation. They are the **causal engine** of the pipeline described in §10.

- **Sexual abuse in care → hypersexual behavior.** Up to 90% of sexually abused teens admit to instigating violent behavior ([Ballard Brief](https://ballardbrief.byu.edu/issue-briefs/sexual-abuse-of-children-in-the-united-states-foster-care-system))
- **Sexual abuse in care → 1.59x risk of risky sexual behaviors** → teen pregnancy → intergenerational foster care ([Ballard Brief](https://ballardbrief.byu.edu/issue-briefs/sexual-abuse-of-children-in-the-united-states-foster-care-system))
- **Mental-health collapse + no treatment → school failure → delinquency → juvenile justice → adult prison** (see §10.3 pipeline data)
- **PTSD + placement instability → runaway → sex-trafficking recruitment** (see §10.5 — 60% of trafficked children came from foster care/group homes)

> **Indictment:** §10 is not a separate tragedy. It is the downstream of §11. The girl sexually abused in a foster home at 13 is the young woman pregnant by 19 and the inmate at 24. The 11-year-old with suicidality is the 16-year-old runaway and the 17-year-old trafficking victim. **These are one story.**

## 11.7 The closer

Every theologian who has ever preached on the problem of evil was, unknowingly, standing next to the answer. The answer is not abstract. It is sitting in a group home in St. Louis. It is a 13-year-old girl whose foster father has a key to her room. It is an 11-year-old boy who has already thought about how he would do it if he ever did it.

The Church's abdication does not produce neutral outcomes. It produces:

- 1 in 3 foster children sexually abused in the system designed to protect them
- PTSD at twice the rate of war veterans
- Suicide attempts at nearly 10x the general adolescent rate
- 80% of alumni not "doing well"

**The Gospel is not only or even primarily about sin management. It is about whose children these are.** The American church has answered that question with its calendar, its budget, and its silence. The answer is not "mine."

329,000 tonight. Their wounds are not abstractions. The receipts are in this section.
