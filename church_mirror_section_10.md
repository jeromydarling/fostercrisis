## 10.3 Prison pipeline — the sharpest cuts

- **~1 in 5 U.S. prison inmates is a former foster child** — ~25x overrepresentation vs. the general population ([CEPR, 2022](https://cepr.org/voxeu/columns/foster-care-prison-pipeline))
- **~70% of former foster youth arrested at least once by 26**; for males, nearly 81% ([Chapin Hall Midwest Study](https://ocfcpacourts.us/wp-content/uploads/2020/06/Midwest_Evaluation_of_the_Adult_Functioning_001015.pdf); [The Imprint, 2023](https://imprintnews.org/top-stories/decriminalize-young-people-who-grew-up-in-foster-care/246740))
- **7% of former foster youth were currently incarcerated at interview** — 14% of males, 1% of females ([Chapin Hall Midwest Study](https://ocfcpacourts.us/wp-content/uploads/2020/06/Midwest_Evaluation_of_the_Adult_Functioning_001015.pdf))
- **~13% entered state prison between 18–23**, vs. national baselines of 2.7% (males) and 0.2% (females) ([Journal of Research in Crime and Delinquency / NIH PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8975219/))
- **Lifetime incarceration rate by age 20: 42%** ([Children and Youth Services Review, 2025](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0145213425002248))
- **A child moved through 5+ placements has a 90% risk of criminal-legal system involvement**; by 17, over 50% already have an arrest, conviction, or detention on record ([Criminal Law Practitioner, American University](https://www.crimlawpractitioner.org/post/the-foster-care-to-prison-pipeline-a-road-to-incarceration))

### Prison entry by foster exit type (WA prospective study, ages 18–23)

| Exit type | % entering state prison by 23 |
|---|---|
| Adopted | 3.2% |
| Guardianship / relative placement | 7.0% |
| Aged out | 8.6% |
| Reunified with family | 13.2% |
| AWOL / runaway | 19.1% |
| Exited to juvenile detention | **48.2%** |

Source: [Foster care, permanency, and risk of prison entry — NIH PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8975219/)

> **Indictment:** A child bounced through five homes is more likely to be arrested than a child born with a loaded gun in their crib. That's not a broken system — it's the system working as designed.

## 10.4 Homelessness pipeline

- **22–30% of youth who age out of foster care experience homelessness during the transition to adulthood** — vs. **4% lifetime rate** in the general population ([Penny Lane synthesis, 2024](https://www.pennylane.org/resources/foster-care-statistics); [AJPH / NIH PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3969135/))
- **31–46% of aged-out foster youth homeless at least once by 26** ([American Journal of Public Health / NIH PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3969135/))
- **~29% of all unhoused youth ages 13–25 nationally have spent time in foster care** ([Alternative Family Services](https://www.afs4kids.org/blog/35-foster-youth-homelessness-statistics-you-should-know/))
- **~75% of unhoused youth in the San Francisco Bay Area came through foster care or juvenile justice** ([Alternative Family Services](https://www.afs4kids.org/blog/35-foster-youth-homelessness-statistics-you-should-know/))
- **61% of homeless former foster youth end up incarcerated** (vs. 46% of homeless youth without welfare history) — homelessness is a prison accelerant for this population ([Alternative Family Services](https://www.afs4kids.org/blog/35-foster-youth-homelessness-statistics-you-should-know/))

> **Indictment:** The American church funded a building program while nearly one-third of children raised by the state ended up sleeping on the street.

## 10.5 Trafficking pipeline — the number that should stop every sermon cold

- **~60% of child sex-trafficking victims recovered in a 2013 FBI nationwide raid (70+ cities) came from foster care or group homes** ([Iowa HHS](https://hhs.iowa.gov/media/6797); [Loyola CLRJ](https://lawecommons.luc.edu/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=1160&context=clrj))
- **86% of likely child sex-trafficking victims reported to NCMEC in 2016 were in the care of foster care or social services** at the time of disappearance ([Loyola CLRJ](https://lawecommons.luc.edu/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=1160&context=clrj))
- **In Connecticut, 86 of 88 identified child sex-trafficking victims had prior child welfare involvement** ([Loyola CLRJ](https://lawecommons.luc.edu/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=1160&context=clrj))
- **LA Probation: 60% of juveniles arrested for prostitution were in foster care** ([Human Trafficking Search / City Limits](https://humantraffickingsearch.org/resource/37-foster-care-and-human-trafficking-statistics/))
- **50–90% of child sex-trafficking victims identified in 2013 had prior involvement with child welfare services** ([Human Trafficking Search / Child Welfare Information Gateway](https://humantraffickingsearch.org/resource/37-foster-care-and-human-trafficking-statistics/))
- **In NYC, 75% of trafficked youth had foster care history; in CA, 50% of children sold into trafficking had prior foster involvement** ([Human Trafficking Search / City Limits](https://humantraffickingsearch.org/resource/37-foster-care-and-human-trafficking-statistics/))

> **Indictment:** When the U.S. government wants to find a child to be raped for profit, the most efficient place to look is the foster system it itself operates. Every pastor who has preached against the sex trade but never called a foster agency is accountable to that fact.

## 10.6 Teen-pregnancy pipeline (the intergenerational engine)

- **71% of young women who aged out of foster care had been pregnant at least once by age 21** — vs. 34% in the general population ([Midwest Evaluation via FosterVA](https://www.fosterva.org/blog/what-percentage-of-girls-in-foster-care-are-pregnant-by-21))
- **By age 19, 55% of aged-out foster females were already pregnant; 23% of males had fathered a child** — pregnancy rates rose **300%** between 17 and 19 ([Children and Youth Services Review / NIH PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3902972/))
- **At 19, foster females are pregnant at 50% vs. 20% in the general population** — a 2.5x gap appearing regardless of maltreatment history, religiosity, school connectedness, or academic achievement ([Dworsky & Courtney / NIH PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3902972/); [Guttmacher Institute](https://www.guttmacher.org/gpr/2011/06/teen-pregnancy-among-young-women-foster-care-primer))
- **Teen parents in foster care are more likely to have their own children placed in foster care**, creating a cycle the system euphemistically calls "continuity of involvement" ([Casey Family Programs, 2024](https://www.casey.org/pregnant-parenting-strategies/))

> **Indictment:** The foster system is manufacturing the next generation of foster children.

## 10.7 Taxpayer price tag

- **Over a 10-year window, the ~300,000 youth who left foster care without transition support cost American society nearly a quarter of a trillion dollars** ([Annie E. Casey Foundation: Cost Avoidance](https://www.aecf.org/resources/cost-avoidance-the-business-case-for-investing-in-youth-aging-out-of-foster))
- **Justice-system costs alone: >$5 billion per annual aging-out cohort of young men** ([Annie E. Casey Foundation](https://www.aecf.org/resources/cost-avoidance-the-business-case-for-investing-in-youth-aging-out-of-foster))
- **Lost earnings / HS dropout: >$2 billion per annual cohort** — foster youth graduate HS by 19 at 58% vs. 87% general population ([Annie E. Casey Foundation](https://www.aecf.org/resources/cost-avoidance-the-business-case-for-investing-in-youth-aging-out-of-foster))
- **Teen parenthood costs: ~$250 million per annual cohort of aged-out young women** ([Annie E. Casey Foundation](https://www.aecf.org/resources/cost-avoidance-the-business-case-for-investing-in-youth-aging-out-of-foster))

Forward extrapolation: **every year America fails to solve this, the bill is ~$7–8B in justice-system-only costs, and the kids pay the non-dollar portion in years of their lives.**

## 10.8 The causal twist that closes the argument

A 2022 NBER quasi-experimental study used near-random assignment of child welfare investigators as an instrument. For the *marginal* child — one whose placement depended on investigator strictness — **foster placement actually *reduced* the arrest rate by age 19 from 37% to 12%, conviction from 35% to 7%, and incarceration from 26% to 5%** ([CEPR / Bald, Chyn, Humlum, Stephenson 2022](https://cepr.org/voxeu/columns/foster-care-prison-pipeline)).

So:

1. Foster youth are catastrophically overrepresented in prison — observational fact.
2. For the marginal child, foster care *reduces* crime risk versus being left home.
3. **Therefore:** the households these children came from were even worse than the system that fails them. The Church let both exist.

There is no interpretation of the data under which the Church comes out looking responsible.
