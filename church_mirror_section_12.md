## 12.1 The thesis

The indictment in §10 and §11 described what foster care does to children while they are still inside it (abuse, mental illness, suicide) and what it does to them shortly after they leave (prison, trafficking, pregnancy, homelessness). This section describes what it does to them **30, 40, and 50 years later.**

The body of a foster alum is a biological ledger. The receipts are legible in their bloodwork.

Every childhood stressor — removal, sexual abuse, placement instability, homelessness, psychotropic medication — is written into the endocrine and cardiovascular systems as **allostatic load**. By age 25, former foster youth already have a chronic-disease burden that looks a decade older than their peers. The American church walked past this wound for generations. The wound is now a coronary.

## 12.3 The definitive prospective evidence — Pediatrics 2014

This is the single most important study in this section. Harvard / University of Washington / Midwest Evaluation researchers prospectively compared **596 former foster youth** to **1,461 economically secure young adults** and **456 economically insecure young adults** from the National Longitudinal Study of Adolescent Health, tracked from late adolescence to ages 25–26 ([Pediatrics 2014 / NIH PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4243069/)).

The headline finding:

> "Former foster youth appear to have a higher risk of multiple chronic health conditions, **beyond that which is associated with economic insecurity.**" — [Pediatrics 2014](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4243069/)

Translation: it isn't poverty doing this. It's foster care.

### Ages 25–26 chronic disease rates by group

| Outcome | Economically Secure | Economically Insecure | Former Foster |
|---|---|---|---|
| Any other chronic health condition | 1.2% | 3.5% | **8.7%** |
| Nonprivate insurance (no insurance) | 19% | 30% | **41%** |
| Medicaid | 5% | 20% | **41%** |

Source: [Pediatrics 2014 / NIH PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4243069/)

Additional findings:

- **BMI of former foster youth was the highest of all three groups** — significantly higher than even the economically insecure group
- When compared to economically secure peers, the foster group had **significantly elevated odds of almost every chronic-disease outcome measured**; only diabetes fell just short of statistical significance (p = .06) while showing the same trend
- The economically insecure group had elevated odds on **only two outcomes** (asthma and smoking) compared to the economically secure — meaning the foster effect vastly exceeded the general poverty effect

> **Indictment:** A child raised in poverty is already at elevated health risk. A child removed from poverty into foster care is at much greater risk. The system that was supposed to rescue them poisoned them slower.

## 12.4 Chronic illness in foster children — even before they exit

The UC Irvine analysis of the 2011–2012 National Survey of Children's Health (Turney & Wildeman, 2016) found that children with foster-care history — compared to children never in foster care — were:

- **7x as likely** to experience depression
- **6x as likely** to exhibit behavioral problems
- **5x as likely** to feel anxiety
- **3x as likely** to have attention deficit disorder, hearing impairments, and vision issues
- **2x as likely** to suffer from learning disabilities, developmental delays, asthma, obesity, and speech problems

Source: [UC Irvine News, 2016](https://news.uci.edu/2016/10/17/foster-care-children-at-much-greater-risk-of-physical-mental-health-problems/)

These elevated rates persisted even after comparison to children from single-mother and economically disadvantaged households — confirming the foster system itself is a causal multiplier, not just a marker for preexisting poverty.

### Obesity — the slow coronary

- **Long-term foster children (ages 2–19) in an LA study: 40% overweight or obese, 23% obese** ([NIH PMC, 2010](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3586377/))
- **Children in group homes: 60% overweight or obese, 43% obese** ([NIH PMC, 2010](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3586377/))
- Compare to the nationally representative NHANES sample (2007–2008): 31.7% overweight/obese
- **Utah study: 18% of children 3–18 entering foster care were already obese; 35% were overweight or obese at intake** ([cited in NIH PMC review, 2010](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3586377/))
- Contributing factor: **psychotropic medications**, which are frequently prescribed to foster children and are associated with weight gain ([NIH PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3586377/))

Adolescent obesity carries a **70–80% probability of becoming adult obesity**, and obesity compounds virtually every other chronic disease including type-2 diabetes, cardiovascular disease, asthma, and several cancers ([NIH PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3586377/)).

> **Indictment:** We medicate foster children into obesity, then send them into adulthood with the coronary risk profile of a 50-year-old. The Church held bake sales for building renovations while the state made the children themselves into casualties.

## 12.5 The ACE mechanism — why adversity becomes disease

The **Adverse Childhood Experiences (ACE) Study**, conducted by Kaiser Permanente and the CDC starting in 1998, is the foundational dataset linking childhood trauma to adult disease. The study found a **dose-response relationship** between ACE count and nearly every major cause of adult death in the United States, including heart disease, cancer, chronic lung disease, liver disease, stroke, diabetes, and depression ([Felitti et al. 1998, via MI DHHS](https://www.facebook.com/michiganhhs/posts/did-you-know-adverse-childhood-experiences-aces-may-be-linked-to-getting-cancer-/1099490215548560/)).

### Virtually every foster child has 4+ ACEs by definition

ACEs measured by the original 10-item questionnaire include: physical abuse, sexual abuse, emotional abuse, physical neglect, emotional neglect, parental divorce, parental mental illness, parental substance abuse, parental incarceration, and domestic violence witnessed. A foster child has, by the very act of being removed, been exposed to the conditions behind multiple ACEs. A child who is placed in group care, moved through multiple homes, and sexually or physically abused in care has 7–10 ACEs before adulthood.

### What 4+ ACEs does to your body

- **JAMA Network Open 2021 meta-analysis (≥4 ACEs vs. none):** **2–3x odds of heart disease, cancer, and respiratory disease; 3–6x odds of mental illness**; significant elevations in dyslipidemia, chronic lung disease, asthma, liver disease, digestive disease, kidney disease, arthritis, and multimorbidity ([JAMA Network Open, 2021](https://jamanetwork.com/journals/jamanetworkopen/fullarticle/2785394))
- **≥4 ACEs:** **2.03x increased odds of multimorbidity** (multiple chronic diseases simultaneously) — regardless of age, sex, education, or income ([JAMA Network Open, 2021](https://jamanetwork.com/journals/jamanetworkopen/fullarticle/2785394))
- **Diabetes risk:** adults with ≥4 ACEs have **~3x the odds of diabetes** in multi-country studies (England, Saudi Arabia, 10-country WHO sample); for every 1-point increase in ACE score, **~11% greater likelihood of type 2 diabetes** ([Current Diabetes Reports / NIH PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5292871/))
- **Cardiovascular disease:** **1.3–1.7x increased risk of ischemic heart disease** per unit increase in ACE score ([JACC Advances, 2025 / NIH PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12145704/))
- **Congenital-heart-disease patients:** each 1-point ACE increase associated with **1.24x odds of composite outcome** (heart failure, stroke, unplanned hospitalization, or ED visit) ([JACC Advances 2025](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12145704/))

### Chronic disease prevalence by ACE score (Journal of Health Care for the Poor and Underserved)

Monotonic (stepwise) increase in disease prevalence with each additional ACE — for depression, COPD, heart attack, stroke, diabetes, cancer, and digestive disease ([NIH PMC 2021](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8462987/)).

### The biological mechanism: allostatic load

- **Allostatic load** is the cumulative "wear and tear" on the body from chronic activation of stress response systems (HPA axis, sympathetic nervous system, inflammatory cascade) ([Biorxiv / Columbia allostatic load study, 2022](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2022.02.22.481548.full))
- Chronic glucocorticoid (stress hormone) exposure causes cells to **accelerate aging markers: shorter telomeres, DNA methylation clocks advanced, reduced cellular lifespan** — biological aging literally measurable at the cellular level ([Biorxiv, 2022](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2022.02.22.481548.full))
- **Pediatric systematic review** (2023): children separated from parents early in life or exposed to ACEs had **significantly elevated allostatic load scores**, predicting worse physical health and working memory deficits into adulthood ([Psychotherapy and Psychosomatics / NIH PMC, 2023](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10716875/))
- Early allostatic load in foster youth mediates the later association between childhood adversity and adult cardiovascular disease, metabolic syndrome, cognitive decline, and accelerated biological aging ([BMJ Open, 2019](https://bmjopen.bmj.com/content/9/7/e030339))

> **Indictment:** Childhood adversity doesn't just hurt the kid emotionally. It rewires the endocrine and immune systems at the cellular level. By 30, a foster alum's body has the wear pattern of a body 10 years older. By 60, they die first.

## 12.6 The comorbidity chain — illness stacks in alumni

The **Casey National Foster Care Alumni Study** (708 African American and white alumni, analyzed by Jackson 2008) documented what comorbidity looks like in this population:

- **98% of foster alumni with depression had comorbid mental illness**
- **94% of alumni with depression had comorbid PTSD**
- **89.9% of alumni with depression had coexisting physical health problems**
- **62% of alumni with PTSD had co-occurring mental disorders; 82% had co-occurring physical ailments**
- **Rates of depression/PTSD comorbidity with physical health conditions were 13–21% higher than with other psychiatric diagnoses**

Source: [Jackson 2008, Casey National Alumni Study / NIH PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3061347/)

### The Northwest Alumni Study on physical/mental health combined

From the Casey Northwest Foster Care Alumni Study (659 alumni, 76% response rate):

- **54.4% had at least one lifetime DSM-IV CIDI mental health disorder**
- PTSD rates approximately 2x those of Iraq War veterans
- Physical health problems rate: "significantly higher" than general-population peers (specific tables in Pecora et al. forthcoming book; [summary at Casey.org](https://www.casey.org/northwest-alumni-study/) and [PDF](https://www.casey.org/media/AlumniStudies_NW_Report_FR.pdf))
- Only **~20% of alumni could be classified as "doing well"** — i.e., the system failed 80%

Source: [Casey Family Programs Northwest Alumni Study](https://www.casey.org/northwest-alumni-study/); [Casey NW Report PDF](https://www.casey.org/media/AlumniStudies_NW_Report_FR.pdf)

### Enhanced foster care demonstrably reduces chronic illness

A 2008 quasi-experimental study (Harvard / Casey) directly compared alumni of a model private foster care program vs. alumni of 2 public programs in Oregon and Washington. The private-program alumni — same demographic, same intake records, same state — had:

- Significantly **fewer** cardiometabolic disorders
- Significantly **fewer** ulcers
- Significantly **fewer** cases of major depression, anxiety, and substance use disorders
- Approximately **50% reductions** in rates of adult major depression and substance use disorders compared to the public-system control group
- **44.7% fewer 12-month mental disorders per 100 respondents** combined across classes

Source: [PubMed, 2008](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/18519820/); [ScienceDaily synthesis](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2008/06/080602160721.htm)

> **Indictment:** When the foster care system is funded correctly — low caseloads, trained workers, good services — chronic illness in alumni drops by ~50%. This proves the disease is not destiny. It's policy. And the Church has been silent on the policy for 50 years while the hospitals fill with its silence.

## 12.7 Health-care access — the cruelest irony

The Pediatrics 2014 data adds one more layer that should end arguments:

- Former foster youth had the **highest rates of Medicaid or no insurance** of any group
- But former foster youth were the **least likely to report not getting needed care** — meaning they are the sickest, the poorest-insured, and yet somehow still receiving less than they need **by their own admission**

Source: [Pediatrics 2014 / NIH PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4243069/)

This means the true chronic-disease gap is almost certainly larger than the measured gap — because foster alumni have less access, less adherence, and less follow-up. What gets diagnosed and recorded is the floor, not the ceiling.

The same child — 13 in a group home, 21 in a prison cell or a pregnancy ward, 45 in a cardiology clinic, 58 at a funeral.

The Church that stood still during §11 is responsible for §10. The Church that stood still during §10 is responsible for §12. **These are not separate sins. They are one abdication, compounding biologically across a human lifetime.**
